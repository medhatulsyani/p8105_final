Statistical Analyses

This page includes the statistical analyses performed to infer the relationship between AED density and traffic volume. The analyses performed were:

  1. Linear regression for mean traffic across boroughs,
  2. Linear regression for mean AEDs across boroughs,
  3. Correlation between AED density and mean traffic volume.

Additionally, a derived metric was created for the Traffic Burden Ratio to highlight the boroughs with the highest traffic congestion and lowest AED coverage.

Pearson correlation between AED density and mean traffic volume

mean_traffic =
  traffic_volume_df %>% 
  group_by(boro) %>% 
  summarize(mean_traffic = mean(vol, na.rm = TRUE))

aed_density = aed_inventory_df %>%
  group_by(borough) %>%
  summarize(total_aeds = sum(aed_num_aeds, na.rm = TRUE))

aed_traffic = mean_traffic %>% 
  full_join(aed_density, by = c("boro" = "borough"))

cor_result =
  cor.test(aed_traffic$total_aeds, aed_traffic$mean_traffic)

print(cor_result)

The resulting Pearson correlation coefficient is 0.826. This indicates a strong positive correlation between AED density and traffic volume, but the p-value is greater than 0.05, indicating it is non-significant.

Derived metric

traffic_summary = traffic_volume_df %>%
  group_by(boro) %>%
  summarize(total_vehicles = sum(vol, na.rm = TRUE))

tbr_df = traffic_summary %>%
  full_join(aed_density, by = c("boro" = "borough"))

tbr_df = tbr_df %>%
  mutate(TBR = total_vehicles / total_aeds) %>%
  arrange(desc(TBR))
